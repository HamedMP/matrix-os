# Future Vision XPRIZE - Competition Rules Summary

Source: https://futurevisionxprize.com/rules

## Overview

The world's largest film competition ($3.5M+ prize pool). Create trailers or short films portraying a hopeful, compelling, technology-forward vision of humanity's future. Presented by Peter Diamandis, XPRIZE Foundation, Google, and Range.

## Key Dates

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| March 9, 2026 | Submissions open |
| August 15, 2026 (11:59 PM PST) | Final submission deadline |
| September 10, 2026 | Script deadline for Top 10 finalists |
| September 25, 2026 | Live presentation in LA at Moonshot Summit |

## Submission Requirements

### Video
- Max 3 minutes + 15-second sponsor trailer (provided after registration)
- Format: MP4 or MOV, minimum 1080p
- Any production approach: live action, animation, AI-generated, hybrid
- English dialogue or subtitles
- General audience appropriate (no explicit violence, language, sexual content)
- 100% original content (no copyrighted characters, worlds, storylines, brands)
- Published via private YouTube link, then made public after review
- Must include #FutureVisionXPRIZE in title or description

### Written Materials (Coversheet - 1 page)
- **Logline**: Story essence in one sentence
- **Synopsis**: 300 words max - brief summary of full film/series concept
- **Personal Statement**: 300 words max - creator's motivation and philosophy

### Treatment
- Up to 12 pages
- Required with video submission
- Top 10 finalists must develop a first draft script by Sep 10, 2026

## Evaluation Criteria (4 dimensions)

1. **Concept quality and execution**: Is the story compelling and well-realized within production constraints?
2. **Scale and ambition**: Does the vision think big enough about humanity's future?
3. **Mission alignment**: Does it genuinely portray a compelling, technology-enabled future where everyone can thrive?
4. **Technology-forward storytelling**: Is advanced technology meaningfully integrated into the narrative?

## Prize Structure

| Prize | Amount | Details |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Grand Prize | $2.5M production equity + $100K cash | Attached producers from Range + Google, script development, talent, financing, Moonshot Summit scholarship |
| Runner-Up (x4) | $100K cash each | Moonshot scholarship, YouTube promotion, Hollywood visibility, Google creative tool credits |
| Top 10 Finalists | $10K cash each | |

- Grand prize winner's film: up to $15M+ total budget (Range + Republic Film Fund)
- Crowd investment campaign via Republic Film
- Google support for production and distribution

## Rights and Exclusivity

- Range gets exclusive rights from submission through Sep 25, 2026
- Range has first right to develop selected projects during competition
- Participants retain ownership of original work
- Google gets producing partner credit + up to 3 EP credits if project goes to feature
- Google provides tools/resources at no cost (subject to availability)

## Engagement

- YouTube metrics (views, likes, comments, shares) factor into evaluation
- View count plays a role but won't give significant advantage to large followings
- Promote widely across social media

## Eligibility

- Open worldwide (except US-embargoed countries)
- Individuals or teams, no size restrictions
- 18+ for prize money (minors can participate with guardian consent)
- No professional experience required
- No existing exclusive/first-look deals that would preclude participation
- Must attend Sep 25 LA event in person to win

## Key Implications for Our Submission

1. **Story first**: They want a FILM concept, not a tech demo. The technology must serve a narrative.
2. **Optimistic and hopeful**: Explicitly stated. No dystopia, no cautionary tales.
3. **Everyone can thrive**: Must show technology benefiting ALL of humanity, not just tech-savvy users.
4. **Producible film**: Top 10 must write a script. The trailer must suggest a full film that could actually be made.
5. **12-page treatment**: We need a real story with characters, arcs, and a world.
6. **YouTube virality matters**: The concept should be shareable, emotionally resonant.
7. **Google partnership**: Winner works with Google. Submission should be compatible with Google's brand (positive, inclusive, forward-looking).
