# YOURS, TRULY -- Visual Storyboard

## How to Use This Document

Each shot has:

- **Frame**: What we see
- **Emotion**: What the audience should feel
- **Message**: What this shot communicates thematically
- **Duration**: Approximate screen time
- **Prompt**: AI image generation prompt (cinematic, realistic)

Production approach: Generate key frames as still images first (Midjourney/DALL-E/Ideogram), then use Google Flow (Veo) to animate them into video clips. Stitch clips in editing software.

---

## ACT 1: THE NOISE (0:00 - 0:55)

### Theme

The exhaustion of modern digital life. Every viewer has lived this. The rhythm accelerates until it becomes unbearable.

### Color palette

Cool blues, harsh whites, screen glow. Desaturated. Slightly overexposed from screen light. Think David Fincher's color grading -- clinical, corporate, oppressive.

### Camera style

Tight close-ups. Shallow depth of field. Handheld micro-movements (anxiety). Fast cuts (1-3 seconds each).

---

### Shot 1.1 -- Morning Notifications

- **Frame**: Close-up of a phone screen lighting up on a bedside table in a dark room. 47 notification badges visible. The blue light casts harsh shadows on rumpled sheets.
- **Emotion**: Dread. The day hasn't started and it's already too much.
- **Message**: Technology ambushes you before you're even awake.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds
- **Prompt**: [SEE PROMPTS DOCUMENT]

### Shot 1.2 -- Dismissing the Avalanche

- **Frame**: Close-up of a thumb frantically swiping away notifications on a phone screen. Notifications keep appearing faster than they can be dismissed. The thumb is a blur.
- **Emotion**: Futility. Sisyphean.
- **Message**: You can never catch up. The system is designed to overwhelm.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 1.3 -- Password Incorrect

- **Frame**: A laptop screen showing "Password incorrect. Please try again." A person's hands hover over the keyboard, frozen. The cursor blinks mockingly.
- **Emotion**: Frustration. A micro-defeat that happens daily.
- **Message**: The tools that are supposed to serve you gatekeep you instead.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 1.4 -- Password Reset Loop

- **Frame**: An email inbox showing "Password Reset Request" from 4 different services. The person clicks one. A form demands: "Must contain 8 characters, one uppercase, one number, one special character, cannot be any of your last 10 passwords."
- **Emotion**: Absurdity. Bureaucratic humiliation.
- **Message**: Security theater that protects the platform, not you.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 1.5 -- Two-Factor Purgatory

- **Frame**: Split composition: phone in one hand showing "Enter verification code", laptop screen showing a spinning loading indicator. The person's eyes dart between both screens. Waiting.
- **Emotion**: Trapped. Held hostage by your own security.
- **Message**: Even logging in requires negotiating with multiple systems.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 1.6 -- Session Expired

- **Frame**: After all that effort, the screen shows: "Your session has expired. Please log in again." The person's hands drop from the keyboard.
- **Emotion**: Defeat. All that work for nothing.
- **Message**: The system doesn't respect your time.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 1.7 -- Cookie Consent Wall

- **Frame**: A beautiful website completely obscured by a cookie consent banner. "We value your privacy" in large text, with 47 toggles. A giant "Accept All" button and a tiny "Manage preferences" link.
- **Emotion**: Cynicism. Dark humor. Everyone knows this is a lie.
- **Message**: "We value your privacy" is the most dishonest sentence on the internet.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 1.8 -- Privacy Policy Stack

- **Frame**: An email inbox scrolled to show a wall of emails, all with subject lines: "We've Updated Our Privacy Policy." From different companies. Dozens of them. Unread.
- **Emotion**: Resignation. Nobody reads these. Everyone knows it.
- **Message**: You've "agreed" to things you've never read, from companies that change the terms whenever they want.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 1.9 -- Terms of Service Scroll

- **Frame**: A finger scrolling endlessly through a Terms of Service document on a phone. The text blurs. The scroll bar is barely visible -- the document is enormous. At the bottom: "I Agree."
- **Emotion**: Surrender. You have no real choice.
- **Message**: Consent without comprehension is not consent.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 1.10 -- Data Breach Email

- **Frame**: A close-up of an email: "Your account may have been compromised. We recommend you change your password immediately." The person stares at the screen. Their reflection is visible in the glass, face lit by the cold blue light.
- **Emotion**: Violation. Helplessness.
- **Message**: You gave them your data. They lost it. You pay the consequences.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 1.11 -- App Overload

- **Frame**: An iPhone home screen with 6+ pages of apps. A finger swipes through page after page. Folders packed with apps. The camera slowly pulls back to reveal the person's face, overwhelmed.
- **Emotion**: Overwhelm. Digital hoarding.
- **Message**: Every problem has an app. Every app is another silo. Another login. Another subscription.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 1.12 -- Subscription Statement

- **Frame**: A bank statement on a laptop screen. The camera slowly pans down a list of recurring charges: Spotify, Netflix, iCloud, Notion, Slack, Figma, Adobe CC, Canva Pro, ChatGPT Plus, Midjourney, Dropbox, Microsoft 365, Zoom... The list scrolls off the bottom of the screen.
- **Emotion**: Slow-building shock. "I pay for ALL of this?"
- **Message**: The subscription economy: you rent your digital life, one monthly charge at a time.
- **Duration**: 4 seconds

### Shot 1.13 -- Cancel Subscription Dark Pattern

- **Frame**: A sequence of modal dialogs: "Are you sure?" -> "We'll miss you!" -> "How about 50% off?" -> "Tell us why you're leaving" (required field) -> Finally, in small gray text: "Cancel subscription." Five steps to leave.
- **Emotion**: Disgust. Being manipulated.
- **Message**: Getting in is one click. Getting out is a maze designed to keep you trapped.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 1.14 -- Copy-Paste Between Apps

- **Frame**: Over-shoulder shot. A person has 4 apps open, split-screened. They select text in one, copy, switch to another, paste, switch to a third, copy something else, switch to a fourth, paste. Their hands move mechanically. Their face shows nothing -- they've been doing this so long they're numb.
- **Emotion**: Numbness. This is what "productivity" looks like in 2029.
- **Message**: Your tools don't talk to each other. You are the integration layer.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 1.15 -- Storage Full

- **Frame**: Phone notification: "Storage Almost Full. Delete apps or media to free space." Below it, the irony: most of the storage is taken by apps the person pays monthly for.
- **Emotion**: Absurdity.
- **Message**: You pay for the software AND it fills up the device you paid for.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 1.16 -- The Breaking Point

- **Frame**: Wide shot. A person at a desk. Laptop open. Phone beside it. The screens glow in the dim room. Their hands are flat on the desk. Still. Not typing. Not scrolling. Just... still. Then they slowly close the laptop. The room goes dark.
- **Emotion**: Exhaustion. Surrender. But also: a decision. Something shifts.
- **Message**: There has to be something else.
- **Duration**: 5 seconds (the longest shot in Act 1 -- the stillness is the contrast)

### Shot 1.17 -- Black

- **Frame**: Pure black screen.
- **Emotion**: Relief. Emptiness. A held breath.
- **Message**: The silence after the storm.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

## ACT 2: THE WHAT-IFS (0:55 - 1:25)

### Theme

Longing. The wishes we all carry about technology but rarely say out loud. Starts digital (search bars, posts), shifts to human (whispers), ends with a child's clarity.

### Color palette

Dark backgrounds. Soft white/warm text glow. Gradually warming from cool blue to amber as we approach the boot.

### Camera style

Static or very slow drift. Clean compositions. Lots of negative space. The visual equivalent of a whisper.

---

### Shot 2.1 -- The First Search

- **Frame**: A Google search bar on a dark screen. A cursor blinks. Someone types slowly: "why do I need an app for everything". The search suggestions appear below, revealing millions of others have asked the same question.
- **Emotion**: Recognition. "I've typed this."
- **Message**: The frustration is universal. You're not alone.
- **Duration**: 4 seconds

### Shot 2.2 -- The Reddit Post

- **Frame**: A Reddit post on a dark background. Title: "I just want to own my own data. Is that too much to ask?" The post has thousands of upvotes. Top comment: "Yes, apparently." Dark humor.
- **Emotion**: Solidarity in frustration. Gallows humor.
- **Message**: Millions of people feel this. The system just doesn't care.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 2.3 -- The Tweet

- **Frame**: A tweet/X post: "14 subscriptions to do my job. this is fine." With a fire emoji and a meme image. Thousands of retweets. The screen slightly pulses.
- **Emotion**: Sardonic resignation. The humor masks pain.
- **Message**: We've normalized the absurdity.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 2.4 -- The Late-Night Text

- **Frame**: An iMessage conversation at 1:47 AM. Dark mode. One message: "i'm so tired of fighting my own computer." Three typing dots appear from the friend... then disappear. No response. Because what can you say?
- **Emotion**: Loneliness. Vulnerability.
- **Message**: This frustration is deeply personal. It affects real relationships, real sleep, real mental health.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 2.5 -- The Search for a Solution

- **Frame**: A search bar again. Someone types: "how to make software that does exactly what I need". The screen begins to fade. The typed text softens, becomes translucent.
- **Emotion**: Hope mixed with futility. They're looking but not finding.
- **Message**: The desire is there. The solution just doesn't exist yet.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 2.6 -- Whisper: Adaptation

- **Frame**: Black screen. Very faint, barely visible: warm golden particles floating slowly, like dust in sunlight. Just texture.
- **Audio focus**: A soft voice, no longer digital: "What if the software adapted to me?"
- **Emotion**: Tenderness. The digital world falls away. This is a person's raw wish.
- **Message**: The transition from typed to spoken marks the shift from digital to human.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 2.7 -- Whisper: Simplicity

- **Frame**: Same dark space, golden particles slightly brighter. A very slow drift.
- **Audio focus**: Another voice: "What if I didn't have to choose between 40 apps?"
- **Emotion**: Weariness. A deep sigh made into words.
- **Message**: The complexity is the problem. Not the features.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 2.8 -- Whisper: Unification

- **Frame**: Particles converging slightly, as if drawn toward a center.
- **Audio focus**: "I just want one place for everything."
- **Emotion**: Simplicity as a radical desire.
- **Message**: The ultimate wish isn't for better technology. It's for less.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 2.9 -- The Child's Voice

- **Frame**: The particles freeze. Then slowly coalesce into a single point of warm light.
- **Audio focus**: A child's voice, clear, unprocessed: "What if the computer just... listened?"
- **Emotion**: Innocence cutting through complexity. The simplest truth spoken by the purest voice.
- **Message**: A child asks the question adults have forgotten how to ask.
- **Duration**: 4 seconds (hold on silence after "listened")

### Shot 2.10 -- The Boot Chime

- **Frame**: The point of light expands gently. A soft chime sounds. A screen begins to glow in a dark room -- we see it from across the room. The warm light spills onto a desk, onto hands, onto a wall.
- **Emotion**: Dawn. Beginning. The answer is arriving.
- **Message**: Something new is starting.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 2.11 -- The Clean Desktop

- **Frame**: The Matrix OS desktop. Close-up. Cinematic shallow depth of field. The screen is nearly empty -- clean, calm, spacious. A dock at the bottom. A subtle gradient background. A cursor blinks softly. A waveform appears -- the OS is listening. The glow of the screen is warm (amber/gold), not cold (blue).
- **Emotion**: Peace. Space to breathe. Permission to begin.
- **Message**: This is what "start from nothing, build everything" looks like.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

## ACT 3: THE FLOW (1:25 - 2:30)

### Theme

Calm power. Technology as an extension of human intent. Each scene answers one of the "what-ifs" from Act 2.

### Color palette

Warm naturals. Golden hour light. Earth tones. Skin tones. The screens glow warm (amber, not blue). Think Spike Jonze / "Her" color grading.

### Camera style

Wider compositions. Longer takes (4-6 seconds). Steady or slow dolly. The camera relaxes when the world relaxes.

---

### Shot 3.1 -- Voice Creates (The First Words)

- **Frame**: Over-shoulder, medium shot. A young man sits at a desk in a warm, naturally lit room. Morning light from a window. He speaks to the screen. We see his mouth move. The waveform on screen responds to his voice.
- **Emotion**: Intimacy. Like speaking to a trusted friend.
- **Message**: The interface is conversation. No menus, no clicks, no learning curve.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.2 -- Software Materializes

- **Frame**: Close-up of the screen. An expense tracker is building itself in real time -- UI elements appearing, a chart drawing itself, data populating. It's not instant (that would feel fake); it takes 2-3 seconds. We watch it form like a timelapse of a plant growing.
- **Emotion**: Wonder. This is the moment that answers "what if the computer just listened?"
- **Message**: Software born from a conversation. Not downloaded. Not installed. Created.
- **Duration**: 4 seconds

### Shot 3.3 -- The File

- **Frame**: A brief shot of the file system. `~/apps/expenses.html`. A real file. The person could open it in a text editor, email it, copy it to a USB drive. It's theirs.
- **Emotion**: Quiet satisfaction. Ownership.
- **Message**: Answers "I just want to own my own data."
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 3.4 -- Iterating by Voice

- **Frame**: Same desk, slightly different angle. The person speaks again: "Make it show a chart by category." The app on screen smoothly updates -- a pie chart appears, animated. The person smiles slightly.
- **Emotion**: Delight. Fluency. Like thinking out loud and watching your thoughts take shape.
- **Message**: Modification is as natural as creation. No code required.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.5 -- Phone Sync

- **Frame**: A phone on a kitchen counter. A clean notification: "Your AI moved your dentist appointment -- traffic delay detected." The person picks up the phone casually while making coffee. Same OS. Same context.
- **Emotion**: Seamlessness. The OS is across devices, not trapped in one.
- **Message**: Answers "I just want one place for everything."
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.6 -- Calendar by Voice

- **Frame**: A person on a couch, relaxed. Speaking casually: "Show me my week." A clean calendar view materializes on a tablet beside them. No app switching. No logging in. Just the answer to the question.
- **Emotion**: Ease. This is what technology was supposed to feel like.
- **Message**: Your OS is ambient. It's there when you need it, invisible when you don't.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.7 -- Multi-Channel

- **Frame**: A Telegram message arrives on a phone. The person replies by voice. The response flows through Telegram but the AI context is the same. A quick cut to: the same conversation continuing on a laptop via the web desktop. Seamless.
- **Emotion**: Freedom. Not locked to one app or device.
- **Message**: Any channel. Same intelligence. Same context.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.8 -- The Child's Game

- **Frame**: A child (8-10 years old) sitting on a living room floor with a tablet. They speak excitedly: "Make me a game where I fly through space!" The screen transforms into a simple but beautiful space exploration game. Stars, planets, a little ship.
- **Emotion**: Pure joy. Magic. This is what children always believed computers could do.
- **Message**: This directly answers the child's voice from Act 2: "What if the computer just listened?" It did.
- **Duration**: 4 seconds

### Shot 3.9 -- Living Software

- **Frame**: Same child, slightly later. The game has changed -- it has adapted to how they play. More asteroids (they like dodging). A score counter (they're competitive). The child doesn't notice the adaptation. They're just playing.
- **Emotion**: Subtle wonder. The software is alive.
- **Message**: Software that evolves with you, not software you adapt to.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.10 -- The Grandparent Call

- **Frame**: An elderly woman (70s) sitting in a warm, sunlit room in Cairo. Plants, family photos on the wall. A tablet on the table beside her. She's on a video call, speaking Arabic. She's laughing. The tablet quietly displays the call information, medication reminders paused until after the call.
- **Emotion**: Warmth. Love. Intergenerational connection.
- **Message**: Technology that disappears. Only the human connection remains.
- **Duration**: 4 seconds

### Shot 3.11 -- "It Knows What I Need"

- **Frame**: Same woman, close-up. The call has ended. She looks at the tablet fondly. Subtitled: "It just... knows what I need." She touches the screen gently, like thanking it.
- **Emotion**: Tenderness. This is a woman who has never "used technology" -- she just speaks, and it helps.
- **Message**: This is what "everyone can thrive" looks like. Not training courses. Not tech literacy programs. A system that meets people where they are.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.12 -- Mexico City Doctor

- **Frame**: A doctor in a clinic, speaking Spanish. "Necesito organizar las citas de mis pacientes." A patient scheduler appears on her screen. She looks up at a patient and smiles -- she's looking at the person, not the screen.
- **Emotion**: Relief. Professional empowerment.
- **Message**: Technology serving the person, not the institution.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.13 -- Rajasthan Farmer

- **Frame**: A man in a field, morning light, holding a phone. He speaks Hindi. A weather and irrigation dashboard appears. Behind him: the crops he's protecting. The phone is small; the impact is enormous.
- **Emotion**: Quiet dignity. Self-reliance.
- **Message**: The same OS that helps a Stockholm developer helps a farmer in Rajasthan.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.14 -- Shanghai Student

- **Frame**: A young woman in a university library, whispering to her laptop in Mandarin. A study planner builds itself around her exam schedule. She smiles and puts in her earbuds, getting to work.
- **Emotion**: Empowerment. Focus.
- **Message**: Different person, different language, different need. Same OS.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 3.15 -- Federation Map

- **Frame**: A stylized visualization: a dark globe with softly glowing nodes in Mexico City, Cairo, Rajasthan, Shanghai, Stockholm, Sao Paulo, Lagos, more. Thin lines of light connect them. Not a corporate network diagram -- organic, like a constellation. The nodes pulse gently.
- **Emotion**: Awe. Scale. Connection.
- **Message**: A federated network of individuals. Not owned by a corporation. Owned by everyone who uses it.
- **Duration**: 4 seconds

## ACT 4: THE CLOSE (2:30 - 3:00)

### Theme

Contrast, then resolution. The visceral before/after. Then the thesis in four words.

### Color palette

The intercuts alternate between Act 1's cold blue and Act 3's warm amber. The title card is clean white on black.

### Camera style

Fast intercuts (1 second each) for the comparison, then slow and deliberate for the statement.

---

### Shot 4.1 -- Intercut: Notifications vs. Calm

- **Frame**: FLASH: phone screen exploding with notifications (from 1.1). FLASH: the clean Matrix OS desktop, one gentle notification (from 3.5).
- **Emotion**: Visceral contrast. The audience feels the difference in their body.
- **Message**: Before and after.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds (1+1)

### Shot 4.2 -- Intercut: Password vs. Voice

- **Frame**: FLASH: "Password incorrect" screen (from 1.3). FLASH: a waveform responding to a voice (from 3.1).
- **Emotion**: Liberation.
- **Message**: From gatekeeping to invitation.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds (1+1)

### Shot 4.3 -- Intercut: Subscriptions vs. File

- **Frame**: FLASH: the scrolling subscription statement (from 1.12). FLASH: the file system showing `~/apps/` -- files the person owns (from 3.3).
- **Emotion**: Ownership vs. rental.
- **Message**: Your software is a file, not a subscription.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds (1+1)

### Shot 4.4 -- Intercut: Isolation vs. Connection

- **Frame**: FLASH: the late-night text message, unanswered (from 2.4). FLASH: the grandmother laughing on a video call (from 3.10).
- **Emotion**: From loneliness to connection.
- **Message**: Technology that connects instead of isolates.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds (1+1)

### Shot 4.5 -- Intercut: App Overload vs. Living Software

- **Frame**: FLASH: 6 pages of app icons (from 1.11). FLASH: the child playing their custom-generated space game (from 3.8).
- **Emotion**: From overwhelm to joy.
- **Message**: From choosing between 40 apps to speaking one sentence.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds (1+1)

### Shot 4.6 -- "Your Software"

- **Frame**: Black screen. Text fades in, clean sans-serif, centered: "Your software."
- **Emotion**: Assertion. Quiet power.
- **Duration**: 2 seconds

### Shot 4.7 -- "Your Data"

- **Frame**: Black screen. "Your data." Below the first line.
- **Emotion**: Building.
- **Duration**: 1.5 seconds

### Shot 4.8 -- "Your Identity"

- **Frame**: "Your identity." The three lines now visible.
- **Emotion**: Crescendo.
- **Duration**: 1.5 seconds

### Shot 4.9 -- "Yours, truly."

- **Frame**: All three lines dissolve. Two words remain, larger: "Yours, truly." The comma is deliberate -- this is a sign-off. The trailer was a letter all along.
- **Emotion**: Resolution and warmth. Not a fist -- an open hand. An invitation.
- **Message**: The thesis. In a world where nothing digital belongs to you, the most radical future is ownership. And this is personal.
- **Duration**: 3 seconds

### Shot 4.10 -- Title Card

- **Frame**: YOURS, TRULY in clean typography. Below: MATRIX OS. Below that: matrix-os.com. Below that, subtle: "Web 4." The screen has the faintest warm glow -- echoing the boot screen from Act 2.
- **Emotion**: Confidence. An invitation.
- **Duration**: 4 seconds
