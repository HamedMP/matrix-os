# XPRIZE Trailer: Concept V1

## Title: "Yours, Truly"

## Logline (draft)

A young man drowning in the chaos of modern technology -- passwords, subscriptions, privacy invasions, app overload -- discovers a different kind of computing: one where software doesn't exist until you need it, and once it does, it's yours.

## The Approach

- **Protagonist**: Young man, shown through hands, over-shoulder, silhouettes, screens -- never a clear face. He is everyone.
- **Visual style**: Mixed media. First half uses real/stock-style footage stitched together (phones, laptops, notification screens). Second half transitions to cleaner, calmer cinematography.
- **Production**: Google Flow (Veo) for AI-generated scenes + screen recordings + motion graphics hybrid.

## Beat Sheet (3 minutes)

### ACT 1: THE NOISE (0:00 - 0:55)

Fast cuts. Anxious energy. Each shot is 1-3 seconds. The rhythm accelerates.

**0:00-0:10 -- Morning assault**
- Phone alarm. Immediately: 47 notifications.
- Hands swiping through a wall of alerts. News, social, spam, app updates.
- Close-up: fingers dismissing, dismissing, dismissing. They never stop.

**0:10-0:25 -- The password hellscape**
- "Password incorrect." Reset email. "Create a new password."
- "Must contain 8 characters, uppercase, number, special character."
- A password manager prompts for a master password. Which was... what?
- Face ID fails. Retry. Retry.
- Two-factor auth. Waiting for the code. Waiting. Waiting.
- Finally in. "Your session has expired."

**0:25-0:40 -- The privacy gauntlet**
- Cookie consent banner. Accept all. Another one. Accept. Another.
- "We've updated our privacy policy" -- email after email.
- Terms of service: 47 pages. Scroll, scroll, scroll. "I agree."
- App permission: "Access your contacts?" "Access your location?" "Access your photos?"
- Data breach notification: "Your information may have been compromised."
- The person stares at the email. Just... stares.

**0:40-0:55 -- The subscription trap + app overload**
- Home screen: 6 pages of apps. Swipe, swipe, swipe.
- App Store: downloading yet another tool. "Free trial (then $9.99/month)."
- Bank statement: Spotify, Netflix, iCloud, Notion, Slack, Figma, Adobe, Canva, ChatGPT Plus, Midjourney, Dropbox... it scrolls off screen.
- Trying to cancel a subscription: "Are you sure?" "We'll miss you!" "Special offer!" 5 clicks to cancel.
- Phone storage full. "Delete apps to free space." Delete the thing you're paying for?
- CRM won't talk to calendar. Calendar won't sync with email. Email won't export to anything useful.
- Copy-paste between 4 apps to do one task.
- Sound design: each cut adds a layer of noise. Pings, buzzes, chimes stacking.

**0:50-0:55 -- The breaking point**
- All sound stops.
- Hands on a desk. Still.
- A breath.
- Screen: the familiar chaos. But the person closes the laptop.
- Black screen. Silence.

### ACT 2: THE WHAT-IFS (0:55 - 1:25)

The bridge. Stillness turns to longing. Every wish is something the audience has thought themselves.

After the silence, soft voices begin. Not narration -- fragments. Overlapping whispers at first, then one at a time, each paired with a visual: text appearing on screen (handwritten feel, different styles, like thoughts materializing) or shown as search bars, forum posts, late-night texts.

**0:55-1:00 -- Silence and first wish (digital)**
- Black screen. The breath.
- A Google search bar appears. Someone types: "why do I need an app for everything"
- The cursor blinks. No results that help. Just ads.

**1:00-1:08 -- The wishes build (digital -> human)**
- More screens in the dark. Each wish starts as something typed, then shifts:
  - A Reddit post: "I just want to own my own data. Is that too much to ask?"
  - A tweet: "14 subscriptions to do my job. this is fine."
  - A search bar: "how to make software that does exactly what I need"
  - A text message to a friend at 1am: "i'm so tired of fighting my own computer"
- The typing sounds fade. The screens dim.
- Now just voices. Soft. No screens. Whispers in the dark:
  - "What if the software adapted to me?"
  - "What if I didn't have to choose between 40 apps that all do half of what I need?"
  - "I just want one place for everything."

**1:08-1:13 -- The turning point (human)**
- Silence. One voice. Clear. A child:
  "What if the computer just... listened?"
- The word "listened" hangs in the dark. 2 seconds.

**1:15-1:20 -- The boot**
- A soft chime. Not a notification. A beginning.
- A screen lights up in a dark room. The glow catches the desk, the hands.
- The Matrix OS desktop. Stylized but recognizable -- a clean surface rendered cinematically. Almost abstract in its emptiness. No clutter. No notifications. No app grid.
- A single cursor blinks.
- Just... space. Ready.

**1:20-1:25 -- The invitation**
- The cursor pulses gently, waiting.
- A waveform appears -- the OS is listening.
- The person takes a breath. About to speak.
- (This is the moment the audience leans in.)

### ACT 3: THE FLOW (1:25 - 2:30)

Calm. Slow. Every shot breathes. Music enters -- warm, building slowly.

**1:25-1:40 -- Voice creates**
- The person speaks (we hear their natural voice for the first time -- answering the wishes from Act 2).
- "I need to track my expenses this month."
- On screen: an expense tracker materializes in real time. Not downloaded. Not installed. Born from the request.
- They speak again: "Make it show a chart by category."
- The app updates, live. A chart appears.
- A brief shot of the file system: `~/apps/expenses.html`. Real. Tangible. Theirs.

**1:40-1:55 -- Seamless life**
- Quick but calm montage of the new reality:
  - Phone notification (same OS, synced): "Your AI moved your dentist appointment -- traffic delay detected."
  - Speaking from the couch: "Show me my week." A clean calendar view appears.
  - "Send the project update to Sara." Done. No app switching. No copy-paste.
  - Getting a message on Telegram. Replying. The same AI, the same context, different channel.
  - Everything is one thing.

**1:55-2:15 -- The human moments**
- A child (sibling/relative): "Can you make me a game where I fly through space?"
  - The OS builds it. A beautiful space game appears. The child plays, delighted.
  - The game subtly adapts to how they play. Living software.
- An elderly parent/grandparent (overseas video call):
  - Clearly not technical. Speaking in their own language (subtitled).
  - Their AI manages their medications, reminds them of appointments, reads their messages aloud.
  - "How's your computer treating you?" -- "It just... knows what I need."
  - Warmth. The technology disappears. Only the human connection remains.
  - (This scene echoes Act 2's "what if the computer just listened?" -- the child's wish, fulfilled for the grandparent.)

**2:15-2:30 -- The world (multilingual montage + federation)**
- Quick cut: the protagonist shares an app with a friend. Drags a file. That's it. Not through a store. Just a file.
- Then: the world opens up. Her-style intimate cuts of people across the globe, each speaking to their OS in their own language:
  - A woman in Mexico City, Spanish: "Necesito organizar las citas de mis pacientes." (I need to organize my patients' appointments.) A medical scheduler materializes.
  - A student in Shanghai, Mandarin: a study planner appears as they speak.
  - A shopkeeper in Cairo, Arabic: an inventory tracker builds itself from conversation.
  - A farmer in rural India, Hindi: a weather + irrigation dashboard.
  - Each shot: 2-3 seconds. Warm. Intimate. The OS responds in THEIR language. No translation layer. No English-first design. It just speaks their language.
  - All subtitled in English.
- Final shot of this sequence: a subtle visual. Connected nodes across a map. Not a corporate network -- a federation of individuals. Each node glows softly. Their AIs coordinating. Encrypted. Private. No corporation in the middle.
- The music swells here -- this is the moment the personal story becomes a global one.

### ACT 4: THE CLOSE (2:30 - 3:00)

**2:30-2:45 -- The contrast**
- Quick intercut: OLD reality (chaos, noise, passwords, subscriptions) vs. NEW reality (calm, voice, flow, ownership).
- Each pair: 1 second old, 1 second new. The difference is visceral.

**2:45-2:55 -- The statement**
- Text on screen, one line at a time:
  - "Your software."
  - "Your data."
  - "Your identity."
  - All three dissolve. Two words remain, larger: "Yours, truly."
  - The comma turns it into a sign-off. The trailer was a letter to the audience all along.

**2:55-3:00 -- Title card**
- YOURS, TRULY
- MATRIX OS
- matrix-os.com
- "Web 4"

## Decided

- **Audio approach**: Minimal voiceover (option B). Act 1 is pure sound design -- pings, buzzes, chimes layering into cacophony. Silence at the break. Only 2-3 spoken lines at key transitions: "What if the computer worked for you?" at the breath, and "Yours." at the close. The protagonist's natural voice appears in Act 3 when they speak to the OS. This restraint makes every word land harder.

## Decided

- **Audio approach**: Minimal voiceover (option B). Act 1 is pure sound design. Act 2 is whispered "what if" voices -- fragments of longing. Act 3 uses the protagonist's natural voice speaking to the OS. Only the closing "Yours." is a deliberate voiceover line.
- **Boot moment**: Stylized + cinematic (B/C hybrid). The real Matrix OS UI, rendered as a clean stylized animation, shot cinematically -- screen glow in a dark room, shallow focus, the light catching hands and desk. Abstract enough to feel like a film, recognizable enough to feel real.
- **Act 2 expansion**: "The What-Ifs" section bridges Act 1 and Act 3. Starts as internet voices (search bars being typed, Reddit posts appearing, tweets) -- grounded in the digital world we just left. Gradually shifts to disembodied whispers as the moment becomes more personal, more human. The final voice is a child, clear and unprocessed: "What if the computer just... listened?" This mirrors the trailer's own transition: from digital fragmentation to human connection.

- **Music**: Hybrid electronic + organic. References: Olafur Arnalds (intimate piano/strings), Hans Zimmer's Interstellar (emotional scale), Max Richter (electronic + classical fusion). Act 1: pulsing electronic unease beneath the chaos sound effects -- not a "song," just texture that makes the viewer's heart rate rise. Clean cut to silence at the break. Act 2: a single piano note when the first wish appears, sustaining and evolving as the wishes build. Act 3: piano deepens, strings enter, subtle electronic warmth underneath -- building slowly from intimate to expansive. Act 4: full swell (strings + piano + synth) for the intercut sequence, then resolves to a single piano chord on "Yours." The music mirrors the story: fragmented noise -> silence -> one note -> harmony.

- **Faces**: No restriction. Protagonist, grandparent, child -- faces are fine. The faceless framing (hands, over-shoulder) is a default but not a rule. Show faces when it serves the emotion, especially in the human moments (grandparent call, child playing).
- **Global expansion**: Brief multilingual montage during the ownership/federation section (2:15-2:30). Quick flashes of people around the world speaking to their own OS in their native language -- Her-style intimate interactions. Spanish (Latin America), Mandarin (China), Arabic (Middle East), more. Each person speaks naturally, the OS responds in their language. Subtitled in English. Shows "everyone can thrive" across cultures and languages without losing the personal core.
- **Grandparent language**: Part of the global texture. The grandparent speaks in their native language (TBD, could be Farsi for authenticity). The multilingual montage in Act 3 expands this further.

## All Design Decisions Resolved

## Why This Wins

1. **Every viewer has LIVED Act 1.** Password resets, subscription fatigue, privacy popups -- this is universal human experience in 2026. Instant empathy.

2. **The silence at 0:55 is the most powerful moment.** When all the noise stops and someone just... closes their laptop. Every person in the audience will feel that in their chest.

3. **Act 3 never explains the technology.** It just shows a person at peace with their digital life. The tech is invisible. That's the point.

4. **"Yours" as a one-word thesis.** In a world where nothing digital is truly yours, the most radical act is ownership. Your software. Your data. Your identity. Yours.

5. **It suggests a feature film.** The treatment expands naturally: what happens when the old system fights back? When corporations resist? When the protagonist has to choose between convenience and freedom? There's a whole film here.

6. **Production is achievable.** Hands + screens + over-shoulder shots + AI-generated scenes + motion graphics. No expensive actors needed. Google Flow handles the cinematic shots. Screen recordings handle the tech demos. Stitched together with strong editing and sound design.
