# Act 4: THE CLOSE -- Image Generation Prompts

## Visual Direction

Act 4 is a series of visceral intercuts that alternate between Act 1's cold, clinical blue and Act 3's warm, golden amber. Same composition concept, opposite emotional temperature. The final sequence is pure typographic minimalism -- white text on black, building to the title card.

**Intercut logic**: Each pair contrasts the problem (cold) and the solution (warm). The cold frames should feel suffocating, compressed, harsh. The warm frames should feel spacious, breathing, alive.

## Production Notes

### Intercut editing (Shots 4.1-4.5)

Each intercut pair should be cut at exactly 1 second per frame (24 frames). The transition should be a hard cut -- no dissolve, no wipe. The visceral contrast depends on the abruptness. Consider a single-frame white flash between the cold and warm frames to heighten the impact (optional, test in editing).

The cold frames should have slightly more contrast and slightly crushed blacks. The warm frames should have lifted shadows and a gentle highlight bloom. This difference in post-processing amplifies the emotional gap even beyond the color grading.

Audio for intercuts: a low, building pulse (like a heartbeat accelerating) that resolves into silence at Shot 4.6.

### Text cards (Shots 4.6-4.9)

These are best created in Figma or After Effects rather than AI image generation, since precise typography control matters. The AI-generated prompts above serve as visual references and mood boards. For production:

1. Create a Figma frame at 1920x1080
2. Use Inter or SF Pro Display font family
3. Background: pure black (#000000)
4. Text: pure white (#FFFFFF)
5. Export as PNG for stills, or build directly in After Effects for animation
6. The fade-in timing is critical: too fast feels cheap, too slow loses momentum. 0.6-0.8 seconds per line is the sweet spot.

### Title card (Shot 4.10)

The radial warm glow can be achieved in After Effects with a subtle radial gradient solid (blend mode: Screen, opacity 3-5%) centered behind the text. Alternatively, use a very soft, large-radius warm light in a 3D compositing setup.

The cascade animation (MATRIX OS > matrix-os.com > Web 4.) should feel like a gentle exhale -- each element arriving with quiet confidence, never rushing.

### Color grading reference

- Cold frames: Teal highlights (#4A8B9E), crushed blacks, desaturated midtones. Reference: Fincher's "The Social Network" or "Gone Girl"
- Warm frames: Amber highlights (#D4A055), lifted shadows (#1A1408), warm midtones. Reference: Jonze's "Her" or Villeneuve's "Arrival" (the warm scenes)
- The contrast between these two grades should feel like two different films intercut together. That dissonance is the point.
